Normally Distributed

As usual, I was doomscrolling through Instagram when I came across a clip from a TV show called Normal People. It was one of the more interesting 1-minute tidbits I had seen, and I was looking to get into a more serious but non-committal show, and as it would turn out, this show was a 12-episode mini-series that received universal acclaim. I sat down and decided to binge this show, and it truly surprised me how much I needed this show at this moment. Spoilers ahead!

The Review

In short, this show follows two Irish young adults, Connell and Marianne, as they play an intricate game of cat-and-mouse to tell each other their feelings for one another as they navigate their own insecurities and coming of age. While this plot has been done time and time again, the beauty of the show is that it is unafraid to let you in on everyone’s flaws while refusing to make commentary on these behaviors itself. The dinner episode shows that Connell and Marianne are in their own world while everyone else is living in it, which leads to this almost surreal layering where Marianne is never really speaking to other characters, merely extensions of Connell.
